LAZY-TIGER · Build & Operate cost overview at 1k to 10k active users

The infrastructure and operating cost of running the LAZY-TIGER member app from launch to ten thousand monthly active users. Drive Each Other, built on a stack that scales with the brand and stays predictable on the bill.

What this is. A cost reference document at four illustrative scale tiers. Infrastructure numbers are facts sourced from the public pricing pages of every named service. Operating ranges are industry benchmarks for similar consumer apps. Nothing in this document constitutes a binding price, a fixed-scope statement, or a final commitment. Scope, timeline, and commercial terms get defined in the engagement proposal stage.
The product

What we're costing

A consumer-grade member app for LAZY-TIGER's three studios (Cardio, Reformer, Yoga). Member-facing, gamified, mobile-first, with live-class leaderboards and performance tracking. Built on the same production stack PROXYZ uses for our own Studio OS, so the engineering patterns are battle-tested.

Tech
What it includes

Class booking, attendance tracking, live in-class leaderboard (Bluetooth heart-rate ingestion via MyZone or Polar, real-time via Pusher or Ably), performance history per member, social feed and achievements, push notifications for class reminders and milestones, on-demand workout video library, member profile and check-in, brand-themed UI.

Web + iOS + Android. React Native via Expo for code sharing, Next.js for the web surface. Authentication, payments, and content all hosted on the same managed infrastructure (Vercel + Neon Postgres + Clerk + Stripe).

Plain English
What members see

A polished app where they book classes, see their workout history, track their progress, and during class see their effort live on the leaderboard. Push notifications nudge them back. The brand looks like LAZY-TIGER everywhere, not like a generic gym app. Members can use it on the web or on their phone, and it stays in sync.

From the founder's side: every member action becomes data you can use. Who's slipping. Who's about to churn. Who to celebrate. Who to invite to the next launch event.

The vocabulary

What "active users" means here

Before reading the cost table, lock in the definitions. The same word means different things at different scales.

Registered
×3 to ×4
People who created an account, ever. Includes lapsed members. Typically 3 to 4 of these per MAU.
MAU
100%
Monthly Active Users. The headline number. Used in this projection. Anyone who opens the app at least once a month.
DAU
~10%
Daily Active Users. Roughly one in ten of your MAU shows up on any given day in a healthy boutique fitness app.
Peak concurrent
5 to 15%
Of DAU. The actual number of people inside the app at the same moment. The number that drives most of the infrastructure cost.
Concrete example

5,000 MAU means roughly 15,000 to 20,000 registered members on the books, ~500 active per day, and 25 to 75 people inside the app at any one moment at peak (think the 6pm class booking window). The cost table that follows is anchored to MAU because that's the metric the team will actually report on.

Read this before quoting the numbers

The four columns are NOT what each tier will cost. They are what each tier can cost with this exact set of services configured at industry-standard plans for a B2C app of this complexity. Real numbers depend on actual session length, real-time message volume, and how aggressively the team uses email and SMS.

A small operator with a tight push-only notification strategy and no video library could come in 30 to 40% under these figures. An ambitious operator running on-demand video, SMS reminders, and AI recommendations from day one could come in 30 to 60% over. Both are valid; both need conscious choices.

The ongoing

What it costs to keep running

Once the app is live, three lines run every month: infrastructure (the table above), engineering hours for bug fixes and small features, and content/support overhead. Numbers below are typical for a healthy boutique-fitness app under active growth.

Infrastructure
From the table above

Pure infrastructure runs $320 to $1,925/month depending on MAU and which optional add-ons are enabled. This is the predictable, automatable line. It grows with usage, not with team size.

Engineering hours
For maintenance and incremental features

Typical engineering hours for a live consumer app of this complexity run $1,500 to $2,500 per month at 1k MAU, climbing to $2,500 to $4,000 per month at 5k MAU. Covers bug fixes, OS updates, dependency upgrades, small features, App Store re-submissions, and the small steady stream of improvements that keeps members engaged.

Content + support
If LAZY-TIGER staff handles it

In-house staff time for member support, content scheduling, push campaign copy, leaderboard moderation. Not a vendor cost, but real time. Budget 10 to 20 hours per month on the staff side once 5k+ MAU.

Plain English summary
All in, every month

The total of infrastructure + engineering hours runs roughly $1,500 to $4,000 per month at launch (1k MAU), $2,500 to $5,500 per month at 5k MAU, and $3,500 to $7,500 per month at 10k MAU. Add staff time on top, but that's measured in hours, not dollars to a vendor.

The AI question

If you add AI features, the bill changes shape

Optional. Many of the most habit-forming features in a modern fitness app use AI quietly in the background: personalized workout suggestions, smart class matching ("you'd love this trainer's reformer class"), AI-generated weekly recap copy, automatic milestone detection ("you just hit 100 cardio classes"). Costs and tradeoffs.

Tech
How AI cost scales

Using Anthropic Claude with a Haiku/Sonnet model split and prompt caching (39% saving on system prompts), a fitness app's typical AI usage runs $0.005 to $0.04 per active user per month for background-only features (no live chat). With a member-facing AI assistant, it climbs to $0.50 to $2 per MAU/month.

At 10k MAU with background AI only: ~$300 to $500/month. With a live AI assistant: $5,000 to $20,000/month and growing with usage.

Plain English
The decision in front of you

Background AI (recommendations, copy, smart matching) is cheap and high-leverage. Members never see "AI" in the UI but the app feels smarter and more personal. This is the recommended starting point.

An AI chatbot that members can talk to is expensive and harder to design well. We'd suggest skipping that until V2 and only adding it if there's a clear member use case that beats the cost.

If AI is enabled, decide who pays

At 10k MAU, an AI feature line can equal or exceed all other infrastructure costs combined. The decision is whether AI is a cost line you absorb, a feature you charge a premium for, or capped at a fixed monthly budget. Pick one consciously, not by default.

The discipline

Debugging at scale, what changes as you grow

Bugs are inevitable. The cost of bugs is not. The difference between a calm operator and a fire-drill operator is in the tooling and the process, not in how senior the engineers are. Here's the standard debugging stack PROXYZ wires in from day one.

01

Error capture

Sentry catches every JavaScript or server error, with full stack trace, environment, user ID, and a timestamp. You see what broke, for whom, when, and on what version.

Sentry Team · $29 to $120/mo
02

Session Replay

For any error or strange user behavior, you can watch a video-like recording of exactly what the user clicked and typed in the 30 seconds before. Bug reports become "I see it" not "I can't reproduce."

Sentry add-on · $3.75 per 1000 replays
03

Distributed tracing

For a slow request, see the full timeline: how long the database query took, how long the AI call took, how long the third-party API took. Knowing where the time goes is half the fix.

Included in Sentry Team
04

Structured logs

Every server action writes a JSON log with userId, sessionId, traceId. Search "what did member X do today" across days. Required for support requests like "I was charged twice, what happened."

Better Stack · $30 to $120/mo
05

Real User Monitoring

Tracks how fast the app actually loads for real members, broken down by device, location, network. Lets you catch "slow on Android in Bangkok during rush hour" issues before they cause churn.

Vercel Speed Insights · $0 to $140/mo
06

Product analytics

What members actually do. Funnel from signup to first class booked. Which features get used and which don't. Catches the class of bug that isn't a crash but is "no one uses this feature."

PostHog · $0 to $50/mo
07

Feature flags + canary rollout

Ship every new feature to 1% of users first. Watch the error dashboard. Expand to 10%, then 100% over a few days. A bad release never reaches everyone. Single biggest cost-saver as the app grows.

PostHog or Statsig · Free tier sufficient
08

Preview deploys

Every change a developer makes spins up its own staging URL with isolated data. Tew, designer, or QA reviews the change on a real running app before it touches production.

Included in Vercel Pro
09

Status page

When something is genuinely down, members see a branded status page with what's affected and an ETA. Stops support overload and protects brand trust during incidents.

Included in Better Stack Nano
The cost of bugs you don't tool for

At 1k MAU, one bad bug costs you a few hours of refunds and apologies. At 10k MAU, the same bug costs you reviews, social posts, churn, and a week of recovery messaging. The full debugging stack above runs $60 to $200 per month at any scale on this list. The math is obvious.

When to add what

The growth ladder · what gets enabled at each scale

Don't pay for everything from day one. Add capabilities as the data tells you to. Below is the typical order PROXYZ recommends for a brand growing through these scale tiers.

At launch · 0 to 1k MAU
→Foundation only. Vercel + Neon + Clerk + Stripe + Pusher + Sentry. The minimum bill that ships a quality app.
→One studio live with real-time leaderboard. Other two as booking-only first.
→Push notifications only, no SMS yet. Cheaper, less annoying, more measurable.
→No on-demand video yet. Test if members want it before building the library.
Healthy growth · 1k to 2.5k MAU
→Add real-time leaderboard across all three studios.
→Turn on PostHog analytics + feature flag system. You'll start needing data to make product decisions.
→Add Resend for transactional emails (booking confirmations, weekly summaries).
→Inngest for background jobs as automations multiply (win-back triggers, weekly digests, achievement detection).
Proven product · 2.5k to 5k MAU
→Add gamification layer (V1.5 build): achievements, streaks, social feed.
→Upgrade Pusher / Ably tier for higher real-time message volumes.
→Vercel Speed Insights on. You now have enough traffic to learn from real user performance data.
→Consider on-demand video if a non-trivial slice of members ask for it. Cloudflare Stream, not Vimeo or self-hosted.
Scale layer · 5k to 10k MAU
→Upgrade Neon to Scale plan for SLA, 30-day point-in-time recovery, read replica.
→Background AI features on (recommendations, smart matching, copy). $50 to $500/mo additional.
→Vercel Advanced Deployment Protection to gate production deploys. Prevents the class of incident where a bad release breaks the app at a peak class time.
→V2 build kicks off: operator dashboards, wearable integrations, multi-location features.
Global push · 10k+ MAU
→Multi-region deployment for users outside Asia.
→Per-market localization (currency, language, time zones).
→Dedicated DevOps support for incident response across time zones.
→Compliance work if expanding to EU (GDPR) or California (CCPA).
