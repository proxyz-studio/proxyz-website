# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Founders and operators of small-to-mid companies in Thailand**, mostly two cohorts touching one bank rail.

- **Thai-owned SMB founders**: 5 to 50 employees, LINE-native communication, Thai-language operations, bank-rail PromptPay, run on a mix of Excel, Line OA, Google Drive, and tacit tribal knowledge. Buy on relationship and demonstration, not on landing page. Audience for AUTOLOOM.
- **Expat-owned Thailand SMB operators**: hospitality (Phuket STR), expat-run wholesale, lifestyle brands. English-first internal ops, Stripe + Notion + Slack stack, Western buyer instincts, willing to read a website end-to-end before booking. Audience for MAGNIZ and the Build-for arm.
- **Partner-founders and exec sponsors**: Cathal-tier ops cos, Tim-tier media ventures, Ko-tier corporate execs at companies like Ananda Development. Sophisticated. Cynical about agencies. Reading the site to decide whether the studio is real, whether the founder can think, whether the engagement modes are honest.

The site speaks to the third cohort first — partner-founders and exec sponsors. The other two cohorts arrive through referral or AUTOLOOM / MAGNIZ surfaces, not through the main proxyz.studio funnel.

## Product Purpose

proxyz.studio is the public face of PROXYZ Studio, a venture-operator studio in Thailand owned by Arnon (Tew) Saksri. It is not a service brochure; it is the studio's resume, manifesto, and audit-booking surface in one editorial document.

The site exists to:

- Make the studio feel undeniable in 30 seconds (one founder, one OS, three live ventures, real installs running today)
- Frame all commercial conversation through four engagement modes (Acquire, Partner, Build with, Build for) and four service capabilities (AI adoption, Automation, EOS/Traction implementation, Growth)
- Route every prospect through a single front door, the free Audit, before any commercial path is named
- Hold the founder's brief and partner gates (Cathal, FAST-FIX, LAZY-TIGER) as live working surfaces, not marketing assets

Success: a partner-founder or exec sponsor lands on the homepage, reads to the Audit CTA without doubt, books a call. A second success: an existing partner or prospect can be sent a link to a single page (LAZY-TIGER, Ventures, Pipeline, the founder's brief) that does the work of a 60-minute meeting in 4 minutes of reading.

## Brand Personality

**Three words**: confident, human, editorial.

**Voice**: declarative. Short sentences welcome. The studio has done the work, doesn't need to oversell. First-person singular ("I") on personal pages (founder's brief), first-person plural ("we") on studio surfaces. Never "we believe", never "we're passionate about". State the thing.

**Tone**: editorial magazine column meets terminal output. Issue numbers ("ISSUE 03 / THE PIPELINE") and section eyebrows ("WHAT WE DO") frame each surface. Hot pink lands as accent, not background. Editorial dark is the default, not a "tools mode" affectation.

**Emotional goal**: a reader closes the tab thinking *"this is run by someone who has shipped"* and *"I know what they would do if I hired them"*. Not delight, not awe, not novelty. Trust through specificity.

## Anti-references

**Reflex-reject aesthetic lanes** (per impeccable's category check):

- SaaS-cream landing pages with feature grids and "Trusted by [logos]" rows. The studio is not B2B SaaS.
- Y Combinator startup landing with the big-claim hero + 3-card "How it works" + pricing tiers. The studio does not have tiers.
- Agency portfolio dark mode with neon gradients, glass cards, and oversized vendored fonts. Too on-the-nose for design-led, signals "we are a design agency", which the studio is not.
- "AI workflow tool" aesthetic (white background + pastel illustrations + Inter Tight). The studio uses AI but is not an AI product.
- Editorial-dark sites that decorate with shine, glow, and parallax for novelty rather than meaning (the founder's brief at `/brief/ananda` walks the line; the rest of the site stays quieter).

**Specific sites that are NOT references**:
- Linear, Vercel, Stripe homepages (product register, not brand register)
- Apple's marketing pages (product hero theatre)
- Bento.me / portfolio template grids
- Crypto / web3 dark mode (neon-on-black)
- Notion's landing (warm cream)
- Most consultancy sites (Bain, BCG, etc., too institutional)

**Closer in spirit (not to copy, to triangulate)**:
- Long-form editorial dark sites in the magazine and journal lane
- Founder-letter sites where the founder is the brand (Tobi Lütke, P.G. style)
- Documentation surfaces that read as authored prose, not autogenerated content

## Design Principles

1. **The studio's resume is its rooms, not its claims.** Every page should let the reader walk through a live working surface (Pipeline page, Ventures detail, founder's brief, partner gates) instead of reading aspirational copy. If a section can be replaced with a screenshot or a live route, replace it.

2. **No SaaS-tier reflexes.** Pricing pages with Basic / Pro / Enterprise are banned, but the deeper rule is broader: no headcount-as-axis, no per-feature checklists, no "get started for free" CTAs. The commercial framing is always engagement modes, never tiers.

3. **Specificity over hype.** Concrete names (AUTOLOOM, MAGNIZ, PRYZM, HERMES, Studio OS, the four modes, the four services) carry the brand. Adjectives ("transformative", "seamless", "robust", "AI-powered") are removed without replacement.

4. **One designed object, not eight pages.** The site reads as a coherent editorial document with surfaces, not as a collection of independent pages. Section eyebrows, issue numbers, the PROXYZ wordmark, IBM Plex Mono everywhere, and the same motion token system unify every route.

5. **Audit is the front door, every path leads back.** Wherever the reader is on the site, the free Audit is reachable in one click. Other CTAs (book a partner gate, read a venture detail, login to Portal) are subordinate.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Target**: WCAG 2.2 AA across all routes. Lighthouse Accessibility 100 on every page per the project's standing rule.
- **Color contrast**: 4.5:1 minimum for body text on dark backgrounds; tinted neutrals (`--fg`, `--fg-mute`, `--fg-dim`) tested against `--bg`. Hot pink `#FF4193` only on dark backgrounds (passes against `#0A0A0A`), never as body text color.
- **Motion**: `prefers-reduced-motion` honored on every animation. Vanta NET and other WebGL effects disabled when set. All entrance reveals collapse to instant opacity changes.
- **Bilingual (EN / TH)**: Thai-language toggle live on homepage. Fallback `[EN]` badges shown on sections without Thai copy. Reading order, font sizes, and tap targets work for Thai script (which can be taller than Latin).
- **Mobile**: hamburger nav at ≤480px (already shipped). All touch targets ≥44px. No hover-only affordances.
- **Keyboard**: full keyboard reachability. Focus rings visible (never `outline: none` without a replacement).
- **Screen readers**: semantic HTML, `aria-label` on icon-only controls (language toggle uses `role="group"`), `role="img"` on decorative badges.
